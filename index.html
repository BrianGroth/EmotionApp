Emotional Compass

Explore emotional spectrums to better understand your feelings and improve communication with others.

Fear → Trust

From anxiety and uncertainty to confidence, security, and faith in others.
Anger → Zen

From frustration and rage to calmness, acceptance, and inner peace.
Sad → Happy

From sorrow and melancholy to joy, contentment, and emotional fulfillment.
Bad → Good

From negative experiences to positive outlooks and favorable outcomes.
Hate → Love

From intense dislike and aversion to deep affection, compassion, and connection.

Communication Tips

  • Express yourself using the format: "I feel _____ when you _____ because _____" and when someone shares with you, respond with "What I heard you say is..."
  • Remember that all emotions are valid — what you and others feel is real.
  • When conversations become heated, pause the debate and ask "How are you feeling right now?"
  • Focus on understanding instead of blaming others for your emotions.
  • Practice humility by acknowledging when you're wrong and how your actions affect others.
  • Give yourself and others permission to experience the full range of emotions.

This tool is designed to help identify, understand, and navigate emotions for better self-awareness and communication.
